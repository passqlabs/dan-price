Why my company runs on a folder of text files

The most useful AI decision I made was also the least technical one.

Everyone building with AI right now reaches for a platform. A framework, an orchestration layer, a vector database, a bespoke app with the model wired into the middle. I went the other way.

The company I founded runs on a folder of markdown files. The agents that do the work read and write plain text. That is most of the architecture, and it was a deliberate choice, not a shortcut. Three things fall out of it.

01  It's AI-agnostic

A file system is the one interface every model already understands. Whatever is best this month reads a folder; whatever is best next month will too. I am not betting the company on one vendor's framework, and the day a better model lands I can swap it in without rebuilding anything.

02  It's legible

There is no database to query and no orchestration engine to reverse-engineer. Anyone can open a file, read the actual state of the business in plain English, change a line, and the next agent picks up the change. The system explains itself.

03  The human stays in the loop by default

Every output is a file I can open and edit before it goes anywhere near a customer. Version control is the audit trail. Nothing happens in a black box.

The lowest-tech option turned out to be the most powerful one. Plain files are the only ground humans and AI agents already share, and building on that beats wrapping a clever cage around the model. Simple turned out to scale better than clever.
